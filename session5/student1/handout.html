🌟 Want To 🎩 Would Like To 🗓️ Going To 💭 Hope To
📐 4 Future Plan Structures
Structure
When to use
Example
want to
Casual desire or personal goal
"I want to travel to Japan next year."
would like to
Polite or formal — interviews, requests
"I would like to find a new job."
going to
Already decided — a firm plan
"I am going to study tomorrow."
hope to
A wish — not certain yet
"I hope to visit Paris one day."
💡 Key Difference
  • going to = you have decided. It WILL happen.
  • hope to = you wish for it. It might happen.
  • want to / would like to = your desire. 'Would like to' is more polite.

📝 Sentence Frames — Use These!
My Plan Sentence Frames
  • "I want to ___ [time expression]."
  • "I would like to ___ in the future."
  • "I am going to ___ [time expression]."
  • "I hope to ___ one day / someday."
  • "I want to ___ because ___."
