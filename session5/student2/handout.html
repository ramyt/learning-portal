Session 5 — Second Conditional

Imaginary, hypothetical and dream scenarios — in fluent English.

💭 If I Were... 🔀 1st vs 2nd 💼 Final 4 Idioms
📐 Second Conditional — Structure
If + past simple , would + infinitive
If I had more time → I would learn the piano.
✅ 1st Conditional — REAL
Possible situation
"If it rains, I will stay home."
"If I get the job, I'll move."
💭 2nd Conditional — UNREAL
Imaginary / hypothetical
"If I won, I would travel."
"If I were a bird, I'd fly."
⚠️ Common Errors — Fix These
❌ "If I would have more money..."
✅ "If I HAD more money..."
Past simple in the if-clause — never 'would have'.
❌ "If I won the lottery, I will buy..."
✅ "If I won, I WOULD buy..."
'Would' in the main clause — not 'will'.
❌ "If I am rich, I would donate..."
✅ "If I WERE rich, I would donate..."
Past simple in the if-clause — 'were' for all subjects (formal).
🎯 3 Uses of the Second Conditional
1. Hypothetical decisions
"If I were the CEO, I would change the company culture immediately."
2. Dream scenarios
"If I had unlimited time, I would travel every continent."
3. Polite requests (softer tone)
"I was wondering if you could help me." / "Would it be possible if I left early?"
💼 Week 2 Final Idioms — 5–8
keep your options open
not make a final decision; stay flexible
"I've kept my options open — I'll wait for all offers before deciding."
think outside the box
approach problems creatively; think unconventionally
"We need to think outside the box — the standard approach isn't working."
a blessing in disguise
something that seems bad but turns out to be good
"Losing that job was a blessing in disguise — it led to something better."
take it with a grain of salt
treat information with scepticism; don't fully believe it
"I'd take that review with a grain of salt — not everything online is reliable."
🥊 Debate Language — Hypothetical Challenges
When someone challenges your hypothetical
  • "That's a fair point, but if we consider..."
  • "I can see that, however if I were in that position..."
  • "Yes, but the problem with that would be..."
  • "If we did that instead, it would cause..."
  • "Having said that, I still believe that if..."
