🔄 Present Perfect vs Past Simple
✅ Present Perfect
"I have been to Beijing." — time is NOT specified
📅 Past Simple
"I went to Beijing last year." — time IS specified
Key rule: Use present perfect when you don't say exactly when. Use past simple when you know the exact time.
⏰ 5 Key Adverbs
ever
In questions — any time in your life?
"Have you ever been to Europe?"
📍 before the past participle
never
Negative — at no time in your life
"I have never eaten snails."
📍 before the past participle
just
Very recently — a short time ago
"I have just arrived home."
📍 between have/has and the participle
already
Before expected — sooner than expected
"She has already finished the report."
📍 between have/has and the participle
yet
In negatives/questions — up to now
"Have you eaten yet?" / "I haven't eaten yet."
📍 at the end of the sentence
