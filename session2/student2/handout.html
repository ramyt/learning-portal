✅ Present Perfect 🔀 vs Simple Past ⚡ Key Adverbs 💬 4 Idioms
✅ Present Perfect — Structure
Formation
Subject + have / has + past participle
I have been to Japan.
我去过日本。
She has eaten sushi before.
她以前吃过寿司。
They have never seen snow.
他们从未见过雪。
Have you ever tried rock climbing?
你有没有试过攀岩？
⚠️ Key Irregular Participles
  • go → been (for places)
  • see → seen
  • eat → eaten
  • do → done
  • try → tried
  • have → had
🔀 Present Perfect vs Simple Past
✅ Present Perfect
General experience — WHEN is not important
"I have been to Paris."
"Have you ever eaten frog?"
🕐 Simple Past
Specific event — WHEN IS mentioned
"I went to Paris in 2019."
"I ate frog last summer."
💡 Quick Test
  • Does the sentence mention WHEN? → Simple Past
  • Is it about life experience, no specific time? → Present Perfect
  • "I have been to..." vs "I went there in 2020." ← spot the difference!
⚡ Key Adverbs
ever
in questions about experience
"Have you ever been to New York?"
never
no experience in your whole life
"I have never eaten durian."
just
very recently — moments ago
"I have just finished my coffee."
already
sooner than expected
"She has already done the homework."
yet
negatives and questions — not yet happened
"I haven't seen that film yet."
💬 4 New Idioms — Week 1 Set
hit it off
to immediately like someone / get along well straight away
"We hit it off straight away — it felt like we'd known each other for years."
go with the flow
to accept things as they happen, without resisting
"I didn't have a plan that day — I just went with the flow."
see eye to eye
to agree with someone / share the same opinion
"We don't always see eye to eye, but we respect each other."
give it a shot
to try something, especially when uncertain about it
"I've never done karaoke, but I'll give it a shot!"
🧠 Fluency Strategies — Extending Answers
📏
Minimum 3 sentences per answer
After "Have you ever...?", never give a one-word answer. Say: yes/no → when/where → how you felt.
🔗
Switch from perfect to past for the story
"I have been to Thailand." → Then switch: "I went there in 2021, and I ate the most amazing food I've ever tried."
💬
Add a discourse marker to every answer
Start with: "Well," / "Actually," / "To be honest," — it buys you time and sounds fluent.
