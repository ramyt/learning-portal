📐 Grammar — Two Correct Patterns
like / love / hate + NOUN or VERB-ing
+ NOUN
I love coffee.
我爱喝咖啡。
+ NOUN
She hates rain.
她讨厌下雨。
+ VERB-ing
I love swimming.
我喜欢游泳。
+ VERB-ing
She hates cooking.
她讨厌做饭。
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • ❌ "I like swim" → ✅ "I like swimming"
  • ❌ "I love eat" → ✅ "I love eating"
  • Add -ing to the verb after like/love/hate

💬 Giving Reasons with 'Because'
Opinion + because + reason
I love coffee because it gives me energy.
我爱喝咖啡，因为它给我能量。
I hate rain because it makes me feel sad.
我讨厌下雨，因为它让我感到悲伤。
I really like swimming because it is relaxing.
我很喜欢游泳，因为它很放松。
I don't like spicy food because it hurts my stomach.
我不喜欢辣的食物，因为它让我胃痛。
📝 Sentence Frames — Use These!
Full opinion sentences
I [verb] ___ because ___.
I really [verb] ___ because ___.
I [verb] ___ a bit but I [verb] ___.
I agree / I disagree because ___.
