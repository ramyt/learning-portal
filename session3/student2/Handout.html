🎯 Hedging 🏗️ Argument Structure 💬 All 8 Idioms
🎯 Hedging Language — Add Nuance
It depends on...
Introducing a condition
"It depends on how you use it. Social media can be positive or negative."
There are two sides to this.
Acknowledging complexity
"There are two sides to this. On one hand... but on the other hand..."
I can see both sides, but...
Balanced → take a position
"I can see both sides, but I still think it does more harm than good."
It's a bit more complicated because...
Adding depth
"It's a bit more complicated because it affects different people differently."
That's a fair point, however...
Acknowledge → counter
"That's a fair point, however the evidence suggests otherwise."
I'd say it's more of a...
Reframing or qualifying
"I'd say it's more of a personal choice than a universal problem."
🏗️ Argument Structure
Claim → Reason → Example → Counter
1
Claim
State your position directly.
"Social media is harmful to real relationships."
2
Reason
Explain why — use: because / since / as
"Because it replaces deep conversation with shallow online interaction."
3
Example
Give a specific example — use: for example / consider
"Consider how couples now sit in silence, both on their phones."
4
Counter
Acknowledge the other side — then rebut
"Of course, some say it connects people. Having said that, this doesn't offset the damage to close relationships."
🥊 Debate Language — Hold Your Position
When challenged — NEVER just say "You're right"
✅ "That's a fair point, but I still think..."
✅ "I can see that, however..."
✅ "Having said that, the fact remains that..."
✅ "I understand your point, but consider this..."
✅ "You raise a good point, although..."
💬 All 8 Week 1 Idioms
1. hit it off
immediately get along with someone
"We hit it off straight away — it felt like we'd known each other for years."
2. go with the flow
accept things without resisting
"I had no plan — I just went with the flow."
3. see eye to eye
agree with someone
"We don't always see eye to eye, but we respect each other."
4. give it a shot
try something new
"I've never tried it, but I'll give it a shot!"
5. under the weather
feel unwell or ill
"I'm feeling a bit under the weather today."
6. get the hang of it
learn how after practising
"English is hard at first, but you'll get the hang of it."
7. break the ice
start a conversation / reduce tension
"He told a joke to break the ice at the start of the meeting."
8. on the same page
have the same understanding
"Let's make sure we're on the same page before we start."
