📐 First Conditional — Structure
If + present simple , will + infinitive
If I get a job → I will move to the city.
❌ COMMON ERROR
"If I will get a job..." — WRONG! No 'will' in the if-clause.
✅ CORRECT
"If I get a job..." — present simple after 'if'.
"If I save enough money, I will travel to Japan."
"She will call you if she has time."
"If it rains, we will cancel the trip."
🔀 Conditional Alternatives
unless
= if NOT / except if
"I'll come unless I'm too tired." (= if I'm not too tired)
as long as
= only if / on condition that
"I'll help as long as you help me too."
provided that
= only if (more formal / written)
"I'll sign provided that the salary is competitive."
💡 Key Rule
  • unless = negative condition — don't add 'not': "Unless I study" NOT "Unless I don't study"
  • as long as = condition must be met
  • provided that = formal written/spoken contexts
💼 Week 2 Idioms — First 4
hit the ground running
start something quickly with energy and enthusiasm
"She hit the ground running — 5 client calls on day one."
bite the bullet
accept a difficult situation with courage
"The salary was low, but I bit the bullet and took the job."
the ball is in your court
it is your responsibility to decide or act next
"I've made my offer. The ball is in your court now."
go the extra mile
do more than what is required; make extra effort
"To stand out at work, you need to go the extra mile."
