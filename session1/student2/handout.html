💡 The Key Insight — Stop Translating
❌ Translation Mode (choppy)
"City is big. I like city. Traffic is bad. Food is many kinds."
✅ Fluency Mode (natural)
"Well, I think big cities have a lot to offer. On the other hand, the traffic can be really frustrating. At the end of the day, I still prefer city life."

Same ideas. Same vocabulary. The difference is connectors and discourse markers.

🔗 8 Discourse Markers — Learn & Use
Well,
Starting / Thinking
Use to start an answer or show you're thinking before you speak
"Well, I think it really depends on the person."
Also use: "Well, to be fair…" / "Well, the thing is…"
Actually,
Correcting / Surprising
Use to add a surprising point or gently correct a previous idea
"Actually, I changed my mind about that recently."
Also use: "Actually, the opposite is true…"
Having said that,
Contrasting
Use to introduce a contrasting or balancing idea
"The job is stressful. Having said that, the salary is excellent."
Similar to: "However," / "But then again,"
What I mean is,
Clarifying
Use to rephrase or clarify something you just said
"It's complicated. What I mean is, there's no simple answer."
Use when you feel your first explanation wasn't clear enough
On the other hand,
Contrasting
Use to give the opposite viewpoint or a different perspective
"City life is exciting. On the other hand, it can be exhausting."
Use when presenting two sides of an issue
To be honest,
Candid Opinion
Use to express a sincere or personal view — often surprising
"To be honest, I find grammar quite boring — speaking is more fun."
Also use: "To be fair," / "Honestly speaking,"
As I was saying,
Returning to Topic
Use to return to a point after a digression or interruption
"As I was saying, the main challenge is finding time to practise."
Use when you got sidetracked and want to continue your main point
At the end of the day,
Summarising
Use to give your final opinion or conclusion after discussing something
"We've discussed all the options. At the end of the day, it's your choice."
Similar to: "Ultimately," / "When all is said and done,"

🧠 Fluency Strategies — When You Get Stuck
⏸️
Use a filler, not silence
Instead of stopping: say "Well…", "Let me think about that…", or "That's a good question — I think…"
🔄
Rephrase if you can't find a word
"I can't remember the word, but it's like a…" / "What I mean is something similar to…"
✏️
Self-correct naturally
Don't stop when you make a mistake. Just say "Sorry, I mean…" and continue. Native speakers do this constantly.
🎯
Don't translate — approximate
If you can't find the exact word, use a related one. "It's a kind of…", "Something like…", "Similar to…"
