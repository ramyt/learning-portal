1
🎧 Independent Listening + Voice Note
Your choice of clip · gist → detail → react → record
30 min
Find a clip yourself — search YouTube for any of these:
• "BBC News short report [any topic you like]"
• "TED Talk 3 minutes [topic you care about]"
• "BBC Learning English 6 minute English"
  • 1️⃣First listen: gist only — what is it about? What is the main point?
  • 2️⃣Second listen: hunt for 3 specific details — numbers, names, examples
  • 💭Inference: what is the speaker's attitude? How can you tell?
  • 🎤Record a 90-second voice note: summarise the clip in 2–3 sentences (YOUR words, not the speaker's), then give your opinion on the main argument
  • 💼Use at least 1 Week 2 idiom naturally in your reaction
  • 📤Send the voice note to your teacher via WhatsApp

🪞 Shadowing Practice — 10 Minutes
Use a clip from today or your own clip
10 min
  • 🎧Pick a 15–20 second segment from any English clip
  • 👂Listen once — absorb the rhythm, don't speak
  • 🗣Play again and shadow immediately — start speaking a half-second after the speaker
  • 🔁Do this 5 times with the same segment until the rhythm feels natural
  • ➕Then find a second segment and repeat

🤝 Tell Your Partner About the Clips
English only · you explain, she asks
15 min
📋 Tonight's paired practice
  • Tell your partner about the 2 clips from today's session — in English
  • Summarise each one: "The first clip was about... The journalist argued that..."
  • She listens and asks 1 question about each clip
  • Then tell her about your own clip from Task 1 — she asks: "Do you agree with that?"
  • Give her a hypothetical reaction using second conditional: "If what they said is true, it would mean that..."
⚠️ Resist the urge to quote the speakers directly. Paraphrase — that is the skill.
