Session 6 — Authentic Listening

Your listening strategy reference. Keep this open during the clips.

🎯 Gist 🔍 Detail 💭 Inference 🪞 Shadow
🎧 The 3 Listening Strategies
🎯
Gist Listening
FIRST LISTEN — big picture only
Don't panic if you miss words. Focus only on the main topic and the speaker's main point. Let unknown words wash over you.
"What is this ABOUT? What is the speaker's main point?"
🔍
Detail Listening
SECOND LISTEN — hunt for specifics
You know the topic now. Listen for: numbers, dates, names, places, examples. You are searching, not translating.
"What facts or examples does the speaker give?"
💭
Inference
HIGHEST SKILL — read between the lines
What does the speaker FEEL? Are they for or against this? What is implied but not said? Look at their tone, word choice, and emphasis.
"What is the speaker's attitude? How can I tell?"
🔊 Connected Speech — Why It Sounds Different
Linking — words run together
Written: "turn it off"
Sounds like: "tur-ni-toff"
Reduction — words get shorter
Written: "going to"
Sounds like: "gonna"
Elision — sounds disappear
Written: "next week"
Sounds like: "nex' week"
Assimilation — sounds change next to each other
Written: "ten people"
Sounds like: "tem people"
💡 Remember
  • These are features, not mistakes — every native speaker uses them
  • When you recognise them, your brain gets faster at decoding real speech
  • Shadowing trains your mouth AND your ears simultaneously
🗣️ Useful Phrases — Summarising & Reacting
Summarising what you heard
"The journalist/speaker argued that..."
"The main point of the report was that..."
"According to the speaker, ..."
"The clip was about... and the key message was..."
Giving your opinion on what you heard
"I found it interesting/surprising that..."
"I agree with the speaker to some extent, however..."
"I would take that with a grain of salt because..."
"If the speaker's argument is correct, it would mean that..."
Signalling what you didn't catch
"I caught the general idea but missed the details on..."
"I understood most of it — the part about X was clearer than..."
"I think they said... but I'm not 100% sure."
🪞 Shadowing — How To Do It
3-Step Shadowing Technique
  • Step 1 — Listen: Play the segment once. Don't speak. Just absorb the rhythm.
  • Step 2 — Shadow: Play again. Start speaking a half-second after the speaker, copying their pace, rhythm, and natural reductions.
  • Step 3 — Reflect: Did you match the rhythm? Were there any reduced sounds you missed? Try once more.
